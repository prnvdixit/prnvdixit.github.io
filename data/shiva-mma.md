His name is Shiva. And his kicks are breathtaking.
There, now you know as much about Shiva as I know about him. These two lines may not seem enough for me to convince you why this is the guy I have known for just 6 months and still he has been the guy I've trusted most in my life EVER.

I have been training alongside him for 6 months now. And well he's my senior in that sense.

All other problems in life seem simpler, because the hardest problem is this guy Shiva whom I am going to meet first thing in the morning and he's going to unleash a barrage of kicks and punches on me. I mean sure I still feel anxious towards that next promotion or launch of that feature I have been leading for last 9 months - but they don't seem like end of world anymore. But Shiva's kicks? Oh they are hell on earth.
Just a couple weeks back, I had my entire back of both thighs coloured blue and green for days. And that was after Shiva was wearing shin guards. Now just think what he'll be like without it.

Martial Arts is where the bully and the bullied both end up.
Even the skinniest person can cause you damage, so martial arts enable you to fight less in a way. It teaches you how less you can damage even with full knowledge of fighting.

It also makes you understand how you've nothing to prove to anyone so you would walk away from fights much more easily.

MMA training and sparring feels like I could finally breathe again even if everything else in my life is crashing over because when I am there, there wass no other choice but to be in the moment. Martial arts have a funny way of contextualising your problems, like yes your personal problems can be annoyed but it still probably won't hurt as much as the 15 year old that's been training since he can walk. I have a hard time focusing on one thing and I tend to overthink a lot and stress myself out, but when I'm in the ring, my mind doesn't wander like that. Swimming is same for me.

I think if you choose a good academy, martial arts can be such a good community because it doesn't matter who you are, if you are showing up and putting in the work even if you aren't the best they will respect you.

It's a no-brainer but I feel martial arts reinforced in me (harshly) that if you really want something, you have to work for it. It also affirmed to me that I was truly always capable of doing something, as long as I at least tried.

Plus, it opened up my eyes to the infinite world of martial arts wisdom, iconic figures like Bruce Lee, Jackie Chan and Abdulmanap really inspired me as I got older to always be patient, modest.
